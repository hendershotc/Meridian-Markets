P/E Ratio
--
The P/E ratio tells you how much investors are paying for each dollar of earnings. Lower can mean the stock is cheaper relative to profits.
EPS
--
Earnings Per Share — how much profit the company made per share. Positive and growing EPS is a healthy sign.
Rev Growth
--
Revenue growth shows how fast sales are growing year over year. Above 10% is generally considered strong.
Margin
--
Profit margin shows what percentage of revenue becomes profit. Higher is better — it means the company keeps more of what it earns.
50d MA
--
The 50-day moving average is the average closing price over the last 50 trading days. Price above this line is considered short-term bullish.
200d MA
--
The 200-day moving average is a key long-term trend indicator. Being above the 200MA is considered a healthy sign for a stock.
Volume
--
Volume vs average — High volume on a price move confirms the move is significant. Low volume moves are less reliable.
Beta
--
Beta measures volatility vs the market. Above 1 = moves more than market. Below 1 = more stable than market.
52w High
--
The highest price this stock has traded at over the past 52 weeks.
